Opportunity Discovery
What these numbers mean
SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market
What a company can realistically earn at Year 5 after launch — after accounting for insurance coverage restrictions, real-world patient adherence, market adoption speed, and approval probability. This is the most important number — used to rank diseases.
TAM — Total Addressable Market
The theoretical maximum — if every eligible patient paid full list price simultaneously. This number is always larger than what you can actually earn, because it ignores insurance restrictions, patients who never fill prescriptions, and competition. Think of it as the ceiling, not the target.
PTRS — Approval Probability
The historical odds of a drug or device reaching FDA approval from its current stage, based on tracking 14,656 programs from 2011–2020. Example: only 1 in 20 cancer drugs that enter human trials ever get approved. Hematology drugs succeed ~1 in 4 times. Medical devices clear ~54% of the time — their bar is lower.
Opportunity / Probability / Value bars
Opportunity — how badly medicine needs a solution here (no treatments = high; crowded market = low). Probability — how likely a drug actually makes it through trials and gets approved. Value — how large the realistic revenue is relative to blockbuster drugs. All three are 0–100.
Tier labels (Exceptional / High / Moderate / Low)
A convenience rating combining all three bars into one label. Exceptional means all three factors are strong simultaneously — rare. High means strong but with one meaningful limitation. Moderate is worth exploring but has clear challenges. Low has significant barriers in at least two dimensions.
Commercial path badge
● Validated market — at least 3 drugs already approved here; the commercial path is proven. ● Viable path — emerging clinical interest with some approvals. ● Emerging — 0 approved therapies but active trial interest. ● Speculative — 0 approved, very few trials; scientifically interesting but no one has successfully built a commercial business here yet.
Scoring methodology

8-criterion MCDA — grounded in EVIDEM v3.0 (Goetghebeur et al., PMC3192743) + Advanced Market Sizing Framework (DisMod · BLP · UCRCD · BIA)
Weights derived from: EVIDEM v3.0 domain consensus · BIO/Informa 2011–2020 success rates · Wong, Siah & Lo (Biostatistics 2019) · Mauskopf BIA (ISPOR) · ICER reference case
Disease Severity & Burden 18%
DALYs (WHO GHO) + mortality + prevalence. EVIDEM Domain 1a. Gillum 2011: DALYs best predictor of research investment.
Unmet Medical Need 16%
Inverse of approved therapies + treatment gap. EVIDEM Domain 1b. Scores 100 when no alternatives exist; 20 when ≥7 approved agents.
Clinical Success (PTRS) 15%
Phase-specific LOA × TA multiplier × biomarker boost. BIO 2011–2020: P1 overall 7.9%, hematology 23.9%, oncology 5.3%. Wong 2019: 13.8%.
Comparative Efficacy 12%
Mechanism novelty + biomarker selection + first-in-class premium. EVIDEM Domain 2. BIO: biomarker-selected programs have 3× LOA.
Economic Value & Revenue 14%
5-stage market sizing: DisMod cascade × Weibull DoT × BLP demand × Bass/UCRCD diffusion × BIA gate. EVIDEM Domain 5. ICER $150K/QALY anchor.
Competitive Dynamics 12%
Pipeline density (CT.gov live) + BLP order-of-entry share + UCRCD cross-WOM. Guseo-Guidolin 2015: b<0 cannibalization, b>0 class effect.
Regulatory & IP Position 8%
FDA designations (Orphan, Breakthrough, QIDP, RMAT) + exclusivity years. EVIDEM Domain 3. Orphan adds 7yr exclusivity; QIDP adds +5yr.
Payer Access & BIA 5%
Budget impact (Mauskopf ISPOR BIA §6): if per-patient cost >$300K or total US spend >$500M, revenue is dampened by logarithmic access restriction.
Score = (Opportunity^0.35 × Probability^0.28 × Value^0.25 × Innovation^0.12) — geometric model prevents any single near-zero factor from masking real opportunity. Confidence interval ±18–23% (Weibull DoT uncertainty + IQVIA 71% forecast error). Sources: EVIDEM v3.0 (PMC3192743) · BIO/Informa 2021 · Wong et al. Biostatistics 2019 · Goetghebeur 2012 MDMA · Mauskopf ISPOR 2012.
